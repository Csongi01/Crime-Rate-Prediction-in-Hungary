About the Hungarian Crime Predictor

A modern university thesis project that uses machine learning to predict crime rates and analyze crime patterns in Hungarian cities with populations over 1000.

Project Overview

The Hungarian Crime Predictor is designed to provide a detailed analysis of crime rates using a dataset from police.hu. This dataset includes public information on committed crimes, such as type, location, year, and crime numbers, covering the years 2009 to 2023.

Features
  • Prediction of crime rates using historical data.
  • Classification of cities based on crime rates.
  • Interactive visualizations and diagrams to illustrate crime trends.
  • Analysis of high-risk areas and factors influencing crime.
Methodology

Using the RandomForestRegressor model, the crime prediction system operates with ~85% accuracy. This accuracy was achieved through detailed preprocessing and data cleaning, which included the following processes:


  • Handling missing values and outliers.
  • Standardizing and normalizing data.
  • Encoding categorical variables.
  • Feature selection to retain only the most relevant attributes.
Significance

The significance of the project lies in its ability to predict crime trends, enabling law enforcement agencies to more accurately determine the probable locations and times of crimes. This facilitates the optimal allocation of resources and enhances community safety. The system allows for the identification of crime hotspots, the planning of crime prevention programs, and the reduction of crime rates in specific towns and communities.
